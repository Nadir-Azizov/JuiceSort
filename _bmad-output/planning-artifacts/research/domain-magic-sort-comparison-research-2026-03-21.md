### Player Complaints (from reviews)

1. **Predatory failure monetization** — Game auto-pops failure screen with purchase offer
2. **Forced restart or pay** — Can't use boosters after failure without paying 900 coins first
3. **Coin economy imbalance** — Difficult to earn enough coins through gameplay alone
4. **Frozen bottles glitch** — "Frozen beakers won't unfreeze even when everything else is sorted"

## Key Takeaways for JuiceSort

### Features to ADOPT from Magic Sort

1. **Shuffle Booster** — High-value gameplay tool, reduces frustration without trivializing puzzles
2. **Decoration Meta-Game** — Gives players a reason to keep playing beyond puzzle completion (retention driver)
3. **Ingredient/Collection System** — Rewards progression beyond stars, feeds into booster economy
4. **Delayed Ad Introduction** — First ~20 levels ad-free to build engagement before monetizing
5. **Smooth Pour Animations** — The #1 thing players praise; must be polished

### Features to AVOID from Magic Sort

1. **Fail-state purchase pressure** — Player reviews consistently hate the aggressive "+1 bottle on failure" popup
2. **Coin-gated boosters** — Requiring coins to use boosters after failure feels unfair
3. **Heavy IAP dependency** — 76% of revenue from IAP works for Magic Sort but contradicts JuiceSort's "Relaxation" pillar
4. **No forced ads** — JuiceSort already has this right; Magic Sort delays but eventually forces

### JuiceSort Competitive Advantages (keep these)

1. **Procedural generation** — Unlimited levels vs Magic Sort's manually-crafted 1000+
2. **City/travel theme** — Unique differentiation; Magic Sort has generic wizard theme
3. **Morning/night moods** — No competitor has day/night atmosphere per level
4. **Star rating system** — Adds replayability that Magic Sort lacks
5. **Star gates** — Progression gating through achievement, not payment
6. **No IAP (ad-only model)** — More player-friendly, avoids top complaint about Magic Sort
