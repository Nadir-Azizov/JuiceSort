# Game Brief: JuiceSort

**Date:** 2026-03-17
**Author:** Nadir
**Status:** Draft for GDD Development

---

## Executive Summary

**JuiceSort** is a relaxing puzzle game where you travel the world, visiting iconic cities and their signature drink spots — sort the drinks to taste the local flavors.

**Target Audience:** Anyone looking for a relaxing mobile puzzle game — core engagement from ages 35-65, but designed for all ages.

**Core Pillars:** Relaxation, Visual Delight, Discovery

**Key Differentiators:** City-based journey with iconic landmarks, day/night mood system (restaurants by morning / bars by night), themed drinks instead of generic colored tubes, emotional travel memories.

**Platform:** Android mobile

**Success Vision:** A published, playable game on the Google Play Store — the first milestone. Growth and optimization come after.

---

## Game Vision

### Core Concept

A relaxing puzzle game where you travel the world, visiting iconic cities and their signature drink spots — sort the drinks to taste the local flavors.

### Elevator Pitch

JuiceSort is a casual drink-sorting puzzle game where every level is a journey to a new city. Sort cocktails in a Parisian bar at night, organize fresh juices in a Tokyo café at sunrise — each location features iconic city landmarks that turn simple puzzles into sweet travel memories. It's the perfect 5-minute escape for commuters and casual players who want more than just colored tubes.

### Vision Statement

JuiceSort transforms the familiar liquid-sorting puzzle into a memorable journey around the world. Players don't just solve puzzles — they explore cities, discover their signature drinks, and collect moments that stick with them long after they put the phone down. Every city feels like a postcard worth revisiting, making JuiceSort the puzzle game people actually remember.

## Target Market

### Primary Audience

Anyone looking for a relaxing, easy-to-pick-up mobile puzzle game. While the 35-65 age group tends to be the most active in this genre, JuiceSort is designed for all ages — younger players enjoy it too. No gaming experience required.

**Demographics:**
- Age: All ages (core engagement typically 35-65, but not exclusive)
- Platform: Android mobile
- Gaming experience: None to moderate — accessible to everyone
- Session length: Short bursts (a few minutes per session)

**Gaming Preferences:**
- Simple, satisfying puzzle mechanics anyone can understand
- No pressure or time limits — play at your own pace
- Visual appeal that makes the game feel premium and inviting
- Welcoming to both first-time players and water sort veterans

**Motivations:**
- Relaxation and stress relief
- A sense of accomplishment from completing puzzles
- The novelty of discovering new cities and their drinks

### Secondary Audience

No specific secondary audience — the game is designed to be universally accessible.

### Market Context

The water sort puzzle genre is well-established on mobile with proven demand and multiple successful titles.

**Similar Successful Games:**
- Water Sort Puzzle, SortPuz, Liquid Sort — all performing well on the Play Store with large install bases

**Market Opportunity:**

- This transforms a commoditized puzzle mechanic into a memorable, personality-driven experience
- Discovery strategy: Google Play Store presence, social media pages, and paid ads — marketing strategy to be developed post-development

## Game Fundamentals

### Core Gameplay Pillars

1. **Relaxation** — The game should never stress the player. No timers, no penalties, no pressure. Calm, soothing experience from start to finish.
2. **Visual Delight** — Every screen should look beautiful and feel premium. Cities, drinks, animations, and UI all contribute to a polished, inviting aesthetic.
3. **Discovery** — Every new level feels like arriving somewhere new. Fresh city visuals, new drinks, day/night moods — players are always curious about what's next.

**Pillar Priority:** When pillars conflict, prioritize:
Relaxation > Visual Delight > Discovery

### Primary Mechanics

- **Pour** — The core action. Players tap and pour liquids from one container to another.
- **Sort** — Organize drinks by color/type to complete each puzzle level.

**Future Mechanics (post-MVP):**
- Explore — Unlocking new cities on the world map
- Collect — Completing cities and earning rewards

**Core Loop:** Select a level → Pour and sort drinks into matching containers → Complete the puzzle → Unlock the next level in a new city

### Player Experience Goals

- **Relaxed and satisfied** after solving each puzzle — a calm sense of accomplishment
- **Curious and delighted** when discovering a new city with its unique drinks and atmosphere
- **Effortless flow** — the game feels intuitive and never frustrating

**Emotional Journey:** Players enter feeling idle or stressed → settle into a calming puzzle flow → finish with a small reward of seeing a beautiful new destination → want to play "just one more level"

## Reference Framework

### Inspiration Games

**Magic Sort**
- Taking: The overall polish — great visuals, satisfying sound effects, smooth gameplay feel. This is the quality benchmark for JuiceSort.
- Not Taking: The generic theme and lack of narrative context — JuiceSort will add what Magic Sort doesn't have.

### Competitive Analysis

**Direct Competitors:**
- Magic Sort, Water Sort Puzzle, SortPuz, Liquid Sort — established water sort games with large install bases on the Play Store

**Competitor Strengths:**
- Solid, proven puzzle mechanics
- Satisfying pour animations and sound effects
- Clean UI and smooth gameplay
- Large level counts keeping players engaged

**Competitor Weaknesses:**
- No story, theme, or narrative — just generic colored tubes
- No sense of journey or progression beyond level numbers
- Every level looks visually the same — no variety in settings
- No emotional connection — players solve and forget

### Key Differentiators

1. **City-based journey** — Each level is set in a real city with iconic landmarks in the background, not just a numbered stage
2. **Day/Night mood system** — Morning levels in restaurants (juices), night levels in bars (cocktails) — same core mechanic, completely different atmosphere
3. **Themed drinks** — Players sort recognizable cocktails and juices, not generic colored liquids
4. **Sweet travel memories** — The game creates emotional connection through city discovery, making players remember and revisit

**Unique Value Proposition:**
JuiceSort is the first water sort puzzle game that turns every level into a travel postcard — combining satisfying puzzle mechanics with a world tour of cities, drinks, and atmospheres that players actually remember.

## Content Framework

### World and Setting

JuiceSort takes place across 38 real-world cities spanning Europe, Asia, Americas, Africa, Oceania, and the Caucasus. Each city features iconic landmarks as background elements with city and country names displayed. Two moods per city: morning (restaurant setting with juices) and night (bar setting with cocktails).

**Launch Cities (38):**
Paris, London, Rome, Barcelona, Amsterdam, Prague, Vienna, Berlin, Istanbul, Athens, Lisbon, Budapest, Tokyo, Bangkok, Dubai, Singapore, Seoul, Hong Kong, Bali, Kuala Lumpur, New York, Rio de Janeiro, Buenos Aires, Mexico City, Havana, Miami, Los Angeles, San Francisco, Cairo, Marrakech, Cape Town, Nairobi, Sydney, Melbourne, Auckland, Baku, Tbilisi, Samarkand

### Narrative Approach

**Environmental + Minimal** — No dialogue, cutscenes, or text-based storytelling. The narrative is told entirely through visuals: city backgrounds, landmark icons, drink themes, and day/night atmosphere shifts. City and country names are displayed for context.

**Story Delivery:** Visual-only — the journey across cities IS the story.

### Content Volume

- 38 cities × 2 moods (morning/night) = 76 unique level environments
- Each city needs: background art, landmark icon/sign, themed drink designs
- Level puzzles scale in difficulty as players progress

## Risk Assessment

### Key Risks

1. **Content Volume** — 38 cities with unique backgrounds, landmarks, and drink themes is ambitious for a solo developer. Risk of burnout or inconsistent quality.
2. **Art Production** — No art/UI experience. Every city needs visual assets. AI tools help but still need creative direction.
3. **First Unity Project** — Strong C# skills, but Unity has its own patterns and learning curve.

### Technical Challenges

- Learning Unity workflows and best practices as a first-time game developer
- Achieving the "Tropical Fresh" visual quality with AI-generated assets
- Smooth pour animations and liquid effects that feel satisfying

### Market Risks

- Water sort genre is crowded on the Play Store — discoverability is difficult without marketing budget
- Even with strong differentiators, competing against established titles with millions of installs
- Marketing strategy not yet defined

### Mitigation Strategies

- **Content volume:** Start with a smaller set of cities for MVP, expand incrementally
- **Art gaps:** Leverage AI tools for asset generation, establish a reusable template per city to speed up production
- **Unity learning curve:** C# experience provides strong foundation — focus on Unity-specific tutorials early
- **Market discoverability:** Build unique city/drink theme as marketing hook — "travel the world" angle is shareable on social media
- **Scope creep:** Stick to MVP (pour + sort mechanics only), defer leaderboards, collections, and explore mode to post-launch
